013/**
014 * Another experimental noise class. Extends PerlinNoise and should have similar quality, but can be faster and has less
015 * periodic results. This is still considered experimental because the exact output may change in future versions, along
016 * with the scale (potentially) and the parameters it takes. In general, {@link #noise(double, double)} and
017 * {@link #noise(double, double, double)} should have similar appearance to {@link PerlinNoise#noise(double, double)},
018 * {@link PerlinNoise#noise(double, double, double)}, and {@link PerlinNoise#noise(double, double, double, double)}, but
019 * are not forced to a zoomed-in scale like PerlinNoise makes its results, have a lower chance of forming shapes with
020 * multiple octaves that appear "non-natural" (such as long straight lines, axis-aligned shapes, and hard angles), and

022 * preferred to other related {@link Noise.Noise3D} or {@link Noise.Noise4D} implementations for most world map

024 * artificial-seeming shapes, which are out-of-place in a world map.
